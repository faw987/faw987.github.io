How Occupancy Grids Work

The environment is divided into a 2D matrix of cells. Each cell stores a probability of occupancy (0 = free, 1 = occupied). Sensor readings from LIDAR, radar, and cameras are fused via Bayesian updating — each measurement either reinforces or weakens the probability of a cell being occupied. The combined map drives the path planner, which finds a collision-free trajectory through free cells.
